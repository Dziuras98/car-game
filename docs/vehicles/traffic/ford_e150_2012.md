## Model-year metadata retained without catalog duplication

The following differences remain mandatory implementation data but do not create additional vehicles:

- **2008:** gasoline-only engine hardware, no RSC on E-150 Commercial, sealed-beam high-tier lamps;
- **2009–2010:** FFV hardware simulated on gasoline, RSC optional rather than standard, aerodynamic high-tier lamps;
- **2011–2013:** FFV hardware simulated on gasoline, standard AdvanceTrac with RSC, aerodynamic lamps; 2012 is the source visual anchor;
- **2014:** FFV hardware simulated on gasoline, standard RSC, sealed-beam high-tier lamp treatment.

## Physics, transmission and audio requirements

Implementation must preserve body-on-frame construction, longitudinal front engine and RWD, Twin-I-Beam front suspension, solid rear axle on dual-stage leaf springs, high centre of gravity, load-sensitive rear behaviour, hydraulic steering and four-wheel disc brakes with ABS. RSC behaviour must follow the selected model year.

The automatic requires a hydrodynamic converter, creep, progressive lock-up, four exact forward ratios, reverse, year/engine-specific final drive, torque and inertia shift phases, kickdown, load/grade scheduling and thermal protection. It must not use a generic six-speed or another transmission architecture.

Both engines are Ford Modular cross-plane V8s but require displacement-specific torque, induction, exhaust and load response. The 5.4L must not be generated only by pitch-shifting the 4.6L.
