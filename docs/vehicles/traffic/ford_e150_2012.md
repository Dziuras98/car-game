## Remaining-model-year research

### 2008

The 2008 model year introduced the final full-body front-end and the revised brakes, steering and suspension. The regular E-150 Cargo Van retained the 4.6L and optional 5.4L gasoline V8s at the same published output anchors used later, but these engines were **not yet FFV**. AdvanceTrac with Roll Stability Control was not offered for the E-150 Commercial Van; its standard baseline therefore uses ABS without RSC. The high-tier cargo-van Exterior Upgrade Package used chrome contour bumpers, a chrome grille surround and dual sealed-beam headlamps, requiring a lamp/material derivative from the 2012 source.

### 2009–2010

For 2009 both the 4.6L and 5.4L became FFV-capable and the redesigned instrument panel was introduced. The regular cargo body remained dimensionally compatible with the source. AdvanceTrac with RSC was optional on gasoline Commercial Vans in 2009 and 2010, so the approved standard-state configuration does not add the optional RSC package. The 2009 Exterior Upgrade Package and 2010 High-Series package use aerodynamic headlamps and chrome exterior treatment compatible with the source theme.

### 2011–2013

The 4.6L and 5.4L FFV powertrains and four-speed automatic continued. AdvanceTrac with RSC became standard equipment on Vans and Wagons. The High-Series package retained aerodynamic headlamps and chrome contour bumpers. The 2012 source is the exact visual anchor for this period.

### 2014

The final full-body model year retained the same published 4.6L and 5.4L FFV outputs, four-speed automatic and standard RSC. The 2014 High-Series cargo-van listing specifies chrome contour bumpers and grille surround with dual sealed-beam headlamps, so 2014 requires a phase-correct lamp/material derivative rather than silently reusing the 2012 aerodynamic lamps.

No additional E-150 regular-cargo engine or transmission family was found for 2008–2014.

## Rear axle and differential policy

Ford towing tables document 3.73 and 4.10 axle ratios for the later E-150, while the 2008 5.4L table also lists 3.55. The approved catalog does not duplicate these choices.

Each approved row receives:

- one standard factory rear-axle ratio verified for that exact year, engine and body;
- an open differential;
- no limited-slip duplicate;
- no tow-package or fleet-governor duplicate.

The common 3.73 ratio is evidenced across the period, but it must not be assumed universally. In particular, the exact standard 2008 5.4L axle may be 3.55 or 3.73 depending on order configuration. The final ratio and axle code remain blocked until a year-specific Ford order guide is retained.

## Chassis and physics architecture

Later implementation must represent:

- longitudinal front engine and rear-wheel drive;
- body-on-frame construction;
- Ford Twin-I-Beam front suspension;
- solid rear drive axle on dual-stage leaf springs;
- commercial-van mass, high centre of gravity and load-sensitive rear-axle behaviour;
- hydraulic power steering;
- four-wheel disc brakes with ABS;
- no RSC for the approved 2008 and standard-state 2009–2010 configurations;
- standard AdvanceTrac with RSC for 2011–2014;
- standard open differential;
- year-specific mass, tyre and control calibration.

A passenger-car suspension, front-wheel-drive van model or one stability-control policy shared across all years is not acceptable.

## Transmission architecture assessment

The four-speed unit is a conventional longitudinal planetary automatic with a hydrodynamic torque converter and lock-up clutch. It requires:

- speed-ratio-dependent converter multiplication and slip;
- idle creep and brake-hold behaviour;
- progressive lock-up and unlock;
- four exact forward ratios and reverse ratio;
- the verified year/engine-specific final drive;
- torque and inertia phases during shifts;
- throttle kickdown and multi-gear selection;
- grade/load-sensitive scheduling;
- thermal protection and high-load shift calibration;
- distinct converter and shift calibration for 4.6L and 5.4L where evidenced.

It must not be approximated by a DCT, automated manual or generic six-speed automatic.

## Engine-audio architecture assessment

Both engines are naturally aspirated Ford Modular cross-plane V8s, but the 4.6L and 5.4L require displacement- and load-specific treatment. Later audio research must establish exact firing order, crank phasing, exhaust grouping, intake/doghouse filtering, SOHC valvetrain and accessory layers, start, idle, loaded acceleration, converter-coupled shifts, overrun, limiter and shutdown.

The 5.4L must not be produced only by pitch-shifting the 4.6L. The 2008 gasoline-only hardware and later FFV hardware may share the architecture but require calibration evidence before their combustion/transient layers are merged.
