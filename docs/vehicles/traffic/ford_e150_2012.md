## Visual identity

The source mesh represents a **2012 Ford E-150 Commercial Cargo Van, regular length, rear-wheel drive**, from the 2008–2014 final full-body E-Series exterior phase.

The source texture and geometry show:

- a solid-panel cargo body rather than a passenger Wagon;
- the regular 216.7-inch body rather than the 236.4-inch Extended body;
- `E-150` rear-door badging;
- standard 50/50 rear swing-out doors;
- 60/40 passenger-side swing-out cargo doors rather than the optional sliding side door;
- solid side and rear panels without cargo-area glazing;
- composite/aerodynamic headlamps, chrome grille surround and bright contour-bumper treatment consistent with the High-Series Exterior Upgrade Package;
- steel wheels / commercial wheel treatment rather than an E-150 Wagon appearance package.

It is not an E-150 Wagon, E-150 Extended Cargo Van, E-250/E-350 Super Duty van, cutaway, stripped chassis, camper conversion or ambulance body.

Identity confidence: **high for E-150 regular-length Commercial Cargo Van and 2008–2014 exterior; high for solid-panel swing-out-door body; medium-high for the exact High-Series package label because the low-resolution source texture does not expose every package detail**.

## Rear axle and differential subdivisions

The Ford towing table evidences **3.73:1 and 4.10:1 rear axle ratios** for E-150 applications with both the 4.6L and 5.4L engines. A limited-slip rear axle was also an available option.

Therefore the strict regular-cargo body can be represented at three levels:

1. **2 base powertrains** when axle choice is treated as an implementation parameter rather than a catalog row;
2. **4 engine/final-drive configurations** when 3.73 and 4.10 are separate;
3. **8 engine/final-drive/differential configurations** when open and limited-slip differentials are also separate.

Across all three E-150 bodies, the equivalent totals are 6, 12 or 24. Exact standard/optional ordering restrictions and axle codes must be retained from the 2012 order guide before final catalog data is committed.

Fleet-only 65-mph and 75-mph speed governors are software options, not separate physical vehicles, and should not become duplicate catalog rows unless the owner explicitly requests fleet-governed configurations.

## Fuel and gaseous-prep subdivisions

### FFV gasoline/E85 capability

Both the 4.6L and 5.4L engines are factory flexible-fuel engines capable of gasoline, E85 or intermediate blends. This is one engine hardware row per displacement, not separate gasoline and E85 vehicles.

Possible policies:

- gasoline-only simulation;
- selectable gasoline/E85 fuel state on one catalog vehicle;
- duplicate catalog rows, which is not recommended because the underlying vehicle is the same FFV.

### 5.4L CNG/LPG Gaseous Engine Prep Package

Ford offered a CNG/LPG prep package for the 5.4L V8. The factory package added hardened intake valves, exhaust valves and valve seats, but it was **preparation for an alternate-fuel upfit**, not by itself a complete tank, regulator and injection system.

If approved, it must be represented as a distinct hardware/upfit configuration with evidence for the completed CNG or LPG system. It must not be implemented as a fuel toggle on an otherwise standard 5.4L without the correct tanks, mass, fuel delivery, output and range effects.

The 50-state Clean Fuel Fleet Certification is not automatically a separate physical row; it becomes one only if retained order documentation proves different engine or emissions hardware/calibration.

## Package and door subdivisions

The following options existed but should not automatically create duplicate vehicles:

- standard 60/40 swing-out side cargo doors versus optional solid-panel sliding door;
- solid panels versus fixed or swing-out side/rear glazing;
- Commercial Van Package;
- Interior Upgrade, insulation, racks/bins and Premium Van packages;
- Mid-Series and High-Series Exterior Upgrade Packages;
- trailer-tow packages;
- 65/75-mph fleet governors.

The **Crew Van Package** is more consequential: it adds a three-passenger second-row bench, restraints, trim, bulkhead and additional mass, and is not available on E-150 Extended Length. It can justify a separate mass/occupant configuration if the owner wants crew vans represented.

The source should keep its regular-length, solid-panel, swing-out-door, high-series exterior identity. Appearance-only package names must not create multiple copies of the same physical calibration.

## Chassis and physics architecture

Later implementation must represent:

- longitudinal front engine and rear-wheel drive;
- body-on-frame construction;
- Ford Twin-I-Beam front suspension;
- solid rear drive axle on dual-stage leaf springs;
- commercial-van mass, high centre of gravity and load-sensitive rear axle behaviour;
- hydraulic power steering;
- four-wheel disc brakes with ABS;
- AdvanceTrac with Roll Stability Control;
- open or limited-slip differential according to approved scope;
- material changes in mass and centre of gravity for Extended, Wagon or Crew Van bodies if approved.

A passenger-car suspension or front-wheel-drive van model is not acceptable.

## Transmission architecture assessment

The four-speed unit is a conventional longitudinal planetary automatic with a hydrodynamic torque converter and lock-up clutch. It requires:

- speed-ratio-dependent converter multiplication and slip;
- idle creep and brake-hold behaviour;
- progressive lock-up and unlock;
- four exact forward ratios, reverse ratio and axle-specific final drive;
- torque and inertia phases during shifts;
- throttle kickdown and multi-gear selection;
- grade/load-sensitive scheduling and trailer/tow behaviour;
- thermal protection and high-load shift calibration;
- distinct converter and shift calibration for 4.6L versus 5.4L if factory data differs.

It must not be approximated by a DCT, automated manual or generic six-speed automatic.

## Engine-audio architecture assessment

Both engines are naturally aspirated Ford Modular cross-plane V8s, but the 4.6L and 5.4L require displacement- and load-specific treatment.

Later audio research must establish:

- exact firing order and crank phasing;
- exhaust manifold and collector grouping;
- intake tract and doghouse/cabin filtering;
- SOHC valvetrain and accessory noise;
- FFV combustion differences if E85 is approved;
- gaseous-fuel combustion and fuel-system noise if a CNG/LPG upfit is approved;
- start, warm idle, loaded acceleration, converter-coupled shifts, overrun, limiter and shutdown behaviour.

The 5.4L must not be created only by lowering the pitch of the 4.6L waveform.
