## Chassis and drivetrain architecture

Normal FWD cars use a transverse front power unit, front transaxle, MacPherson-strut front suspension and multi-link rear suspension. Engine/transmission-specific mass, springs, dampers, steering, tyres, brakes and stability-control calibration remain mandatory.

Japanese i-ACTIV AWD rows require a front transaxle, power-transfer path, prop shaft, electronically controlled rear coupling/final drive and driven rear half-shafts. They must not be represented as FWD vehicles with an arbitrary traction multiplier.

The BY hybrid requires a dedicated battery and power-split hybrid chassis/mass model. It is not the normal 2.0 petrol with an electric torque overlay.

## Transmission architecture assessment

- SKYACTIV-MT requires a real dry clutch, six-speed gearset, exact forward/reverse ratios, final drive, synchronizer and engine-braking behaviour.
- SKYACTIV-DRIVE is a six-speed planetary torque-converter automatic with high lock-up coverage. It requires converter multiplication/slip, creep, progressive lock-up, exact ratios, hydraulic shift phases, kickdown and thermal behaviour.
- The regional four-speed Activematic row, if retained after evidence closure, is a distinct older planetary automatic and cannot reuse six-speed SKYACTIV-DRIVE ratios or scheduling.
- The hybrid electronic CVT is a power-split planetary hybrid transaxle with two motor-generators and no belt/pulley CVT. It requires engine/motor speed coordination, electric launch, regeneration, battery limits and blended braking.

## Engine and driveline audio architecture

Required families include 1.5/2.0/2.5 SKYACTIV-G naturally aspirated direct-injected fours, regional MZR MPI four, 1.5 single-turbo diesel, 2.2 sequential/two-stage turbo diesel, i-ACTIV AWD driveline layers and hybrid combustion/electric transitions.
