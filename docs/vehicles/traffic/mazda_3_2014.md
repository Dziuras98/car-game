## Chassis and transmission requirements

Normal rows use a transverse front power unit, MacPherson-strut front suspension and multi-link rear suspension. The AWD rows require a real front transfer path, prop shaft, controlled rear coupling/final drive and driven rear half-shafts; they may not be simulated by increasing FWD traction.

SKYACTIV-MT requires a dry clutch and exact six-speed ratios. SKYACTIV-DRIVE requires a hydrodynamic torque converter, high-coverage lock-up, planetary gearsets and real shift phases. Activematic is a separate four-speed automatic. The hybrid e-CVT is a two-motor power-split planetary system, not a belt CVT and not a conventional automatic with electric torque added.

## Engine and driveline audio architecture

The retained rows require distinct first-principles families rather than one generic Mazda inline-four waveform:

- **SKYACTIV-G P5/PE/PY petrol** — naturally aspirated high-compression direct-injection inline-four architecture with calibration- and displacement-specific combustion pulse, injection/mechanical detail, intake resonance, exhaust routing, inertia, idle and high-load response. The 1.5, 2.0 and 2.5 families may share validated low-level SKYACTIV-G utilities, but each displacement/output row requires a separate profile where torque, intake/exhaust or limiter behaviour differs;
- **MZR Z6 1.6 petrol** — older port-injected inline-four family with Activematic driveline character. It may not use the SKYACTIV-G primary waveform merely because both are inline-fours;
- **SKYACTIV-D 1.5** — dedicated low-compression common-rail diesel inline-four with injection staging, turbo/VGT response, diesel mechanical orders, engine-braking and governor behaviour;
- **SKYACTIV-D 2.2** — separate larger-displacement sequential/two-stage turbo diesel family with stage-transition, injection, intake/exhaust and 150/175-PS calibration differences. It must not be produced only by lowering or pitch-shifting the 1.5 diesel;
- **Axela SKYACTIV-HYBRID** — combined Atkinson-cycle petrol engine, two motor-generators, inverter and power-split planetary/transaxle audio. Engine start/stop, motor-only motion, blended power, regeneration and engine-speed decoupling from road speed must follow the hybrid state rather than a conventional gear/RPM assumption.

Manual, SKYACTIV-DRIVE, Activematic, i-ACTIV AWD and hybrid power-split rows require distinct clutch/converter, shift, coupling, geartrain and regeneration layers driven by truthful runtime telemetry. The hybrid must not play a permanently idling combustion loop and must expose motor/inverter/reduction sound in electric operation.

Every player row uses an explicit architecture-correct live backend. Each AI family must select either a committed baked bank generated from that backend or live synthesis with a representative opponent-fleet budget. No transmission type may implicitly select an unrelated engine waveform.
