## Research boundary

## Chassis and drivetrain architecture

All normal FWD rows use a transverse front power unit, front transaxle, MacPherson-strut front suspension and torsion-beam rear axle. Each row requires its correct mass, tyres, brakes, springs, dampers, steering and stability-control calibration.

The Japanese e-4WD row is not a mechanical prop-shaft AWD system. It requires a front combustion transaxle plus the evidenced electrically driven rear-axle traction-assist hardware, power supply and control limits. It must not be represented as permanent AWD or a generic torque split.

## Transmission architecture assessment

- Five-speed manuals require a driver-operated dry clutch, exact ratios, reverse, final drive, engine braking and synchronizer behaviour.
- Four-speed automatics require a hydrodynamic torque converter, creep, multiplication, lock-up, exact planetary ratios, shift phases, kickdown and thermal behaviour.
- The CVT rows require genuine continuous ratio control and their actual launch device; simulated Sport steps are control behaviour, not a separate physical gearbox.
- Demio EV requires a complete motor, inverter, battery, regenerative-braking, thermal and fixed-reduction model rather than a combustion transmission locked in one ratio.

## Engine and driveline audio architecture

Required families include standard MZR/Miller/SKYACTIV naturally aspirated petrol fours, MZ-CD diesel fours, e-4WD electric rear-drive contribution and the complete Demio EV electromagnetic/inverter/reduction sound. Miller-cycle and SKYACTIV-G rows may not be represented only by pitch changes to the standard 1.3 MZR, and diesel/EV rows require dedicated architectures.
