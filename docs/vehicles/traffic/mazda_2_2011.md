## Chassis and transmission requirements

Normal FWD rows use a transverse front power unit, front transaxle, MacPherson-strut front suspension and torsion-beam rear axle. The e-4WD row requires a front combustion transaxle and its real electrically driven rear-axle traction-assist hardware; it must not be represented as a mechanical prop-shaft AWD system or generic permanent torque split.

Five-speed manuals require a real dry clutch and exact ratios. Four-speed automatics require a hydrodynamic converter, creep, multiplication, lock-up and planetary shift phases. CVT rows require genuine continuous-ratio control and their actual launch device. Demio EV requires a complete motor, inverter, battery, regenerative-braking, thermal and fixed-reduction model.

## Engine and driveline audio architecture

The approved rows require these separate first-principles families:

- **MZR ZJ-VE/ZJ-VEM 1.3 petrol** — port-injected inline-four combustion, intake/exhaust and mechanical architecture with calibration-specific idle, inertia and load response. The Miller-cycle ZJ-VEM requires its own valve-cycle/combustion and CVT operating profile rather than only an EQ change;
- **MZR ZY-VE 1.5 petrol** — separate displacement and intake/exhaust calibration family for global, North-American and Japanese Sport outputs. The output rows may share validated MZR timing utilities but require distinct profiles when induction, exhaust, limiter or load response differs;
- **SKYACTIV-G P3-VPS 1.3** — direct-injected high-compression petrol architecture distinct from the port-injected MZR waveform;
- **MZ-CD/Y4 1.4 diesel** — small common-rail turbo-diesel inline-four with its own injection, turbo, mechanical, exhaust and engine-braking character;
- **MZ-CD/Y6 1.6 diesel** — separate larger-displacement common-rail diesel family. It must not be produced only by pitch-shifting the 1.4 diesel;
- **Demio EV** — non-combustion motor, inverter, fixed-reduction, regeneration and tyre backend with no idle combustion loop.

The e-4WD row additionally requires an independently controlled rear electric-motor/gearset layer driven by actual assist torque and rear-motor speed. It must not be represented by generic AWD driveline noise or a mechanical prop-shaft layer.

Manual, 4AT and CVT variants require distinct clutch/converter/continuous-ratio and transaxle layers. CVT audio must follow real engine-speed/ratio behaviour; simulated Sport steps are controller events over the continuous mechanism. Player variants use explicit live synthesis, while AI uses a committed baked bank generated from the correct family or a live backend with a representative fleet budget.
